Why Pleks

Two things the others don't do. We built the product around them.

Every other claim on this page — the automation, the statements, the rent collection — falls out of getting these two right. Everything else is table stakes.

01 RECORDS · KEPT

When the question gets asked, the answer is already on file.

Inspections remember when and where they were taken. The deposit clock tracks its own deadline. Every credit check is tied to the consent that allowed it. Every letter, every signed lease, every change to a unit is filed in the order it happened — and exported in one click when a Tribunal, an attorney, or a new staff member needs to see it.

  • AInspections that hold up. Photos carry their own time and address. A tenant can't argue the date when the camera knows it.
  • BDeposit clock, built in. 14 days no claim, 21 days with one. Every deduction itemised; wear-and-tear separated from damage so you're never deducting what you shouldn't.
  • CConsent on file before every credit check. No "we never agreed to that" three months later. POPIA-compliant by default — not after you've trained your staff for an afternoon.
  • DOne file per tenant. One file per unit. Whoever handled it, whatever month it was — it's all in the same place, in the order it happened.
  • EArrears letters drafted for you. Reviewed by you before they leave. Tone steps up by stage — reminder, demand, pre-Tribunal — so the right letter goes out at the right time.
02 SCREENING · ECONOMICS

Applicants pay. You get a FitScore, not a 40-page credit report.

Applicants submit their details, consent, and pay for their own credit check directly. We run the bureau, ID, and deeds checks and hand you a single decision-ready number with a plain-English rationale attached. You don't pay for a check that goes nowhere. You don't read a credit report at 9pm on a Sunday.

  • AApplicants pay, not you. Joint applications billed once. The check happens before they take up an afternoon of your time.
  • BFitScore 0–100. One number combining credit history, affordability, tenancy references, and ID integrity — with each component shown separately so you can see what tipped the score.
  • CEvery applicant shown, every time. Low scores aren't filtered out. The decision is yours to make — and the record proves it was yours.
  • D30% affordability rule flagged automatically. No guessing what "marginal" means when income-to-rent gets close to the line.
  • EShortlist to signed lease — no re-typing. Accept an applicant and the debit order mandate, lease draft, and move-in inspection are queued for you.

The Pleks Charter

Your data is yours. Your landlord's money is theirs. We're just the filing cabinet — and it opens from both sides.

Eight commitments we've put in writing, not in marketing copy. Every one exists because the alternative has bitten someone in this industry. Every one is enforceable — by you, by your tenants, by the Information Regulator.

  1. 01 · TRUST MONEY

    We never hold your landlord's money.

    Client funds stay in your Section 86 trust account, at your own bank, under your own FFC. Pleks has no outbound payment rail — and the codebase can't grow one without rewriting the schema, the lint rules, and the UI layer.

    Enforced at 4 layers · zero custodial authority by design

  2. 02 · TENANT DATA

    We never hold your tenant's bank details either.

    Rent moves tenant-bank to your-bank on a mandate their bank holds against yours. We observe the reconciliation — we don't run the rail. If Pleks vanished tomorrow, the debit orders would keep running. No PCI footprint on the tenant side, no NAEDO, no DebiCheck.

    No inbound payment rail, either

  3. 03 · PORTABILITY

    You can leave any month. We'll help you pack.

    Full export on demand — every lease, tenant, inspection photo with original EXIF, statement, mandate, audit entry. Signed, hashed, timestamped. No contract gate, no exit fee, no “premium migration” tier. Your portfolio was always yours.

    PDF + JSON + ZIP bundle · SHA-256 manifest hash

  4. 04 · ACCESS CONTINUITY

    Overdue doesn't lock you out.

    If we ever need to chase you for an invoice, we'll chase the invoice. Nobody's rent roll becomes the collateral. You can see, export and migrate your data regardless of account state — contractually, not conditionally.

    Written into every Operator agreement

  5. 05 · RIGHT TO BE FORGOTTEN

    Your tenant's erasure request actually works.

    When a tenant asks to be forgotten, we execute it within 30 days — with the narrow statutory carve-outs (FICA 5 years, PPRA trust records, Tribunal holds) disclosed before they submit, not after. No silent retention, no “anonymised” workaround.

    Retention-aware cascade · carve-outs disclosed pre-submission

  6. 06 · NO TRAINING, NO SELLING, NO TRACKING

    You're not a dataset. Your tenants aren't a dataset.

    Our AI provider runs zero-retention on every call: prompts and responses exist for the round-trip, then they're gone. We've deliberately not deployed Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics, or any other product-analytics vendor on the authenticated product.

    Anthropic zero-retention DPA · no third-party analytics, ever

  7. 07 · AGENCY ISOLATION

    Your applicants don't inherit another agency's history.

    FitScore uses that applicant's four declared inputs — affordability, credit, rental history, employment stability. Outcomes aren't pooled across agencies. Your judgement isn't diluted by someone else's mandate, and your book isn't leaked into someone else's model.

    Zero cross-org data aggregation anywhere in the product

  8. 08 · BREACH POSTURE

    If something goes wrong, you hear within 24 hours.

    POPIA gives us 72. We've committed to 24 in every Operator agreement, and we publish the post-mortem publicly. Your tenants' path to the Information Regulator is open and signposted on every privacy page — we don't get to stand between them and the complaint.

    24-hour breach notification · IR escalation path always surfaced
